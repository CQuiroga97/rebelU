United States

American English is the national language of the United States. Although there is no official language at the federal level, most states have declared English as the official language.In certain U.S territories there are native languages that have been recognized in addition to English, such as Spanish, Hawaiian, Sioux, Samoan and Chamorro.

Canada

A multitude of languages are used by Canadians, with English and French (the official languages) being the mother tongues of approximately 56 percent and 21 percent of Canadians, respectively. Other provinces have no official languages as such, but French is used as a language of instruction, in courts, and for other government services, in addition to English.
